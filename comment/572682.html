Aloha Matterhorn,

For me, ''the tailblock tells the tale'', on this board.      In or around late may of 1965, we stopped using a Balsa strip between the GLASS TAILBLOCK, and the foam where it mounts to the board.    The fin in it's present form, appears to be a reshape of the original ''standard''  D  fin.      This was commonly done to existing boards, as higher aspect ratio fins came into vogue.    So,  my conclusion is, that your board dates from sometime in 1964, to early/mid 1965.      I'm sure there is a number on the board, but every so often, one of the shapers would forget to put the number on the board.     Me among them.     I hope that is helpful.

My first board was a second hand Hansen. It had no tailblock but it did have a fin that was the reverse colors of this one. Black core and white sides. I also modified the fin similar to what is seen here. It had a typical D fin and I reshaped it by hand with a hacksaw and sandpaper. Lots of work when you have no power tools.  ;)
