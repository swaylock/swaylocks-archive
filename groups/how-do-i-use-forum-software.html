aloha annie. id say before jumping into any strenuous physical activity such as surfing after having one hip replaced and the other waiting in the wings,it may be a good idea to speak with your doctor first. and let me be clear, i am in no way qualified to give you any sort of medical advice. what i can say with absolute certainty is that its NEVER too late to ride a wave. are you a good swimmer? whats your comfort level in the ocean? may be a good idea to swim as much as possible  before you come down, but not neccesary. i can say for sure youre gonna love it! ive taught dozens of people over my 29 years of surfing and not one left the water frowning. and i would be more than happy to take you surfing, i have several big boards that i use to teach friends, family, the neighborhood kids etc. waikiki beach has gentle waves well suited to learning, so does barbers point- white plains beach which is just a short drive from my home. if you would prefer paying a professional there are lots to choose from. there are beach concessions  along waikiki beach where you can rent the board and get lessons from a beach boy. but my recommendation, if you insist on going that route would be hawaiian fire. an outfit run by honolulu fire fighters. pretty class act from what im told. heres the url to their website http://www.hawaiianfire.com/about_us.htm by the way, riding waves does not have to be a stand up surfing experience,. bodyboards are a blast too and far more accesible and safer if the surfing looks scary or things dont go well. i again offer my friendship and some surf lessons, a bar-b-que lunch on the beach. you can call me rusty. my e-mail is: houdinihoudini@rocketmail.com. aloha annie, and ahuihou!

     Howzit makakilo, You should have been here 10 years ago when just about all 50 members were all veteran board builders and we would just exchange tricks and tips that we knew about and there wasn't that many people who joined to learn to make boards and that took a couple of years before that started. Now we have over 15,000 members and things have changed a lot but I really enjoy passing on all the things I learned in over 45 years of working with resin and 40 years of building boards. There was a time when if you wanted to make surfboards you had to learn on your own which meant lots of problems and mistakes or you got lucky and got a job working in a factory and hopefully they kept you on and taught you how to make boards by watching and learning till they let you work yourself up the ladder to being a shaper or a laminator. One about Sways is there aren't much going on in the ego dept and we are all fairly humble about our skills.
