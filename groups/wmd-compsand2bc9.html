I searched high and low for it to no avail but I did register on the forum for the show and I'll ask to see if the host can post a photo of the jig. It was a pretty simple setup that had an adjustable fence that could be set at any angle enabling the veneer to be just hanging over the edge. He then placed a similar shaped triangle of plywood over top of the veneer so that it was a jig base/veneer/plywood sandwich with the veneer hanging just slightly proud of the other pieces. This enabled him to run a hand plane down the side of the jig and just true up the edge of the veneer perfectly straight. The whole assembly, fence and all, clamped down to his workbench.
