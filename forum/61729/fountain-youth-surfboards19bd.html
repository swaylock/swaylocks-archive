I haven't bee on on Sway's for a bout a year, mostly moved over to Jamboards TBH.

But I've been busywith reconfiguring my Business Model to wr for where I'm at now.  

I turned 70 four days after Christmas and that fact alone blows my mind.  Time flies when you're haing fun..... in fact pages turn in the book of Life faster than most of us would prefer. 

I've survived my wife's passing and learned how powerful a force GRIEF can affect a person.  There was a point my body rebelled from all the stress and demans in the aftermath of her passing in August 2020.  I ended up not being able to sleep in bed and was relegated to an office chair I had...... this lasted for 3-1/2 months, and thank god I had plenty of experience globetrotting while flying the world stand by as a "non rev" passenger with Mel who was retired Flight Crew for tWA then AA once they swallowed them up.

I'd just pretend I got stuck in a middle seat in Coach versus the more than usual lucky First Class or Business Class we sually enjoyed.  

Life finds a way...... my doctor listened to me and pretty much agreed that the symptoms I was suffering from were in large part due to the stress of recent events.  Tests didn't reveal any red flags.  To make a long story short, a resolution came about in the most unexpected way. 

I had told about the Disney+ tv show "/Wandavison" and decided to check it out.  It was a novel, rather imaginative bit of entertainment.  Whiile tuning in one night, the episode involved the wife losing her sister (I think it was her sis) and she is engulfed with grief.  Her husband is a robot, albeit somewhat lifelike, but still I guess what some peole call a synth for synthetic.

Anyway, I'm sitting there watching this show, and he says to her something like "you know honey, I can't possibly know what you are feeling, I mean, after all I'm a machine, but I can empathize with you..... but I can tell you this GRIEF IS LOVE PERSERVERING....

 GRIEF IS LOVE PERSEVERING................ it hit me like a ton of bricks.  Epiphany time.

Up until that time, I was in that camp agreeing that whoever said "Time Hels All Wounds" was completely full of crap.  There were many people grieving the loss of a loved one for months, years, decades, a lifetime......... 

GRIEF IS LOVE PERSEVERING.......................... OF COURSE IT IS!

I give thanks to the writer who was so insightful as to help me thru this challenging patch of my life!  His/Her statement allowed me to park that pain in its rightful place. I was now able to "compartmentalize" my loss......... to lace it safely stored in a very special place to cherish for ever THEN MOVE ON WITH MY LIFE.

I shared this story with my doctor and told him within two weeks the sciatica that had been plaguing me abated and was nearly all but gone. It reminded me of the moment Maverick goes supersonic to a heavily outnumbered dogfight in the 1st Top Gun, then gets unsettled and withdraws at a critical moment........... "TALK TO ME GOOSE".......... 

That Wandavision writer was MY GOOSE.

I got back into the fight that Life sometimes presents, and I relish it and am kicking ass oonn it.  I don't have time to be "old".

For the last few months I've been scheduling & doing what i call "Production Custom Runs" of my Models.  This has helped keep the crew at Haakenson's with a steady flow of work including some finish shaping by guys I trust & admire.  

By giving these guys work, I am able to help them meet their monthly expenses while having some income that can allow them to further their creative aspirations while promoting their own label.  I am always in support of other shapers as well as Haakenson Fiberglass, Arctic Foam and U.S. Blanks as pillars in our industry.  

All custom orders continue to be shaped by me personally, & depending upon the nature of the order, they are either shaped from a 'Raw' or 'Machined' blank.

Arctic has cut my files for years & the high number of cuts keep the blanks very close to finish allowing me to maintain a high degree of Desigin Integrity.  Consistency is king when it comes to Reverse Engineering, and I only justify the approach when you are doing Models and have the Volume to justify doing so.

Models are an identifier that virtually PROMISES the way a certain Design will ride, by being consistent the Designer is making good on his/her PROMISE that the board will ride per the Model's claim or description.

I am fortunate that my designs have been widely embraced & I enjoy a strong repeat clientele as well as many BF Quiver Builders.  I take that as a blessing, a reward for smart & hard work always endeavouring to treat my customers like gold.  In the past few months I've amassed a "Built Ahead, Ready to  Buy" Inventory where Domestic purchases can be shipped in 24 to 48 hrs. from time of purchase.  International takes a bit longer partly due to present day logisitics & carry over challenges from Covid. 

The BFV8's have continued to grow in popularity & are now being ridden all over the world in waves I never dreamed possible......... we built 80 recently with sizing running as small as 5'6" and as long as 9'0".

The Rail Chined Single to Double Concave Design loves trimming high up on the Wave Face then Swooping Down with Speed to Spare for Career Making Bottom Turns. Pump this Sled thru a Series of S Turns for Section Making Speeds.

Testimonial #29117: "

Ride Report for a 6'4" Tri Plane Hull:

Hey Bruce I have great news! Finally got that TPH into some good waves. The problem with getting a board in May is summer happens next and it's just been too crap to warrant taking that board out. Paddled it out in clean 3-4' Sunset Cliffs this morning and boy does that board move. I'd say the most surprising experience was the sensation of effortless glide that I got when up in plane. You know that experience when you're screaming down the line in plane but there's no sound of the board moving through water? That. For a guy that surf primarily single fin convex equipment that was most welcome. It was so novel I was tempted to just stand there until I remembered I had some turns to do. The second surprise was that I LOVE this board as a thruster. I honestly can't even guess the last time I rode tri fin. After a couple sessions as a quad I felt I wanted a pivot point to tame all that V. I will say I was a little worried that it was TOO much V after those sessions. Not at all! Stable, fast, easy to surf, and nimble in turns, color me impressed. Thanks Bruce!!
