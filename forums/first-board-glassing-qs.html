I've recently completed my board #1 and most of my questions were answered by making lots of mistakes.  Its really the best way to learn, adapt on your feet.   Once you start glassing you'll see all sorts of solutions and new ideas will flood in guaranteed!  Plan on wasting about half your resin on the floor and in unnecessary batches, so get extra.

The amount of glass used depends on a lot of things.  First of all different fiberglass cloths have different weihghts.  6oz (per sq yd) and 4oz are typically used with surfboards.  A standard Polyurethane shorty with polyester resin is glassed with 4oz bottom and 2x 4oz deck.  It depends on the materials you are using, the dimension of the board etc. etc. lots of variables!

Greetings. Firstly, the standard way to glass a board is to do the bottom layer of fibreglass first, let cure, take down the laps and then glass two layers of fibreglass at once on the deck and then let cure. Hot coating in polyester resin terms is a second coat of resin applied to the board once fibreglassing is complete. Polyester hot coat resin is just normal polyester resin with sanding additive added to it. It should fill in the weaves completely and completely cover the fibreglass so you don't hit the fibreglass when you sand it. Unless you oversand of course. In terms of epoxy, hot coating is either the same resin again or the same resin again with sanding additive. You usually apply hot coat resin with a brush by the way.

Gday Jack,

Ive just come out the other side of what you are about to do....It seems a bit scary at first especially if you have produced a shaped blank that your proud of, but if i could give you one piece of advice, that would be to have a go.

I glassed a 6'8''SB using 1 layer of 6oz mat on the bottom and a 6oz deck with a 4oz layer below it and used a poly resin

Yeah I wasted a heap of resin but its a learning process which you have go through too.

I glassed the bottom lam first using 850ml of resin catalysed with mekp  at the ratio of 100:0.75 resin to mekp and it kicked in about 15mins, the temp was about 27degC.

This could have been a problem but fortunately I was working thee resin quickly and my laps had already been wrapped. I was left with a few  dry spots in the lap but wet them down later with some more resin.no problem....

Once cured, I flipped the board and cut my weave and prepared the deck( sanded a few burs from the tail and nose to smooth off with 80grit and a sanding block).

I used the same recipe for resin/catalyst but increased my quantity to 1.25L due to the extra layer of mat. The temp was a bit lwr 25degC which bought me a little extra time. This went fairly well but was left with an air bubble under the lam as big as an aussie 5 cent piece.. The swalockers advised me well on the remedy and repair was successful...

The hot coat saw 100:1 mix applied with a brush   top first then bottom........ fins..... well this process may vary if you glass on,used fcs or any other fin system. Make your choice and use the wealth of info in these discussions as I did...Research, get prepared, make a plan then go for it. I hope your results were as pleasing as my first attempt.

Sand with care.good luck..

Cheers nadles 

PS get some really good shears to cut your weave...it will really help
