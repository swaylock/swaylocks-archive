The ultimate tool for grinding laps is a good old wood rasp with very coarse grit and/or 40/60 grit on a hard pad with the sander.

Nothing and I mean NOTHING beats a right angle die-grinder with 3-M diskits.  The diskits can be bought at Wal-Mart, Lowes or most any automotive body supply shop.  They come in two types "screw-on" and "snap on"  I prefer the screw on because they are the most commonly stocked in the afore-mentioned stores.  Even if you use a small compressor and have to wait on it, the die-grinder is still the best.  Habor-Freight for the best deal on a die grinder.  Fast, clean and even, more importantly ---- FLAT.  I used a Dremel with a barrel for a long time.  Very tricky technique.   Dremel makes a right angle attachment, but I could never find disks that would work on it.  Once I bought a nice small Chicago Pnuematic right angle die grinder at Harbour-Freight I never looked back.  The one I bought doesn't have the "safety" on the trigger, more convenient.  You can drop the disks in a bucket of acetone for awhile and they will clean right up with a small wire brush.  They last really long this way.  Alot of people are resistant to using one.  But I'm tellin' you this is what the Pro-Glass Shops use and for good reason;  they do the best job.  Lowel
