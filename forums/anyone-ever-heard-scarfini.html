Wow, he might be flirting with disaster... if FCS gets wind of this, they might come after him legally. The plugs are obvious moulded copies of FCS plugs.

Legal issues aside, resin by itself is often brittle. Often additives (cabosil, colloidal fillers, fiber mill, etc) are mixed in to create an internal 3-D lattice to strengthen the glass. If you want to test this out and these were freebies and/or you have an odd number of them and don't mind banging one up, drop it from shoulder-height onto concrete and see if it chips/shatters. If you choose to do so, please report back.

Ok! Update on the scarfini crash test results!
I dropped one plug from onto a stone floor once, and it survived!!!
I dropped it again, and it survived!!!
I dropped it once more, and once again it survived!!!
Resistant they are!
From touch they didn't feel like resin to me (at least pure resin) so i talked to the sales person from the company that sells them (she lives near me). They're made of resin, but not only from resin, they're a mix of acrylic and resin, more resin than acrylic.
The only other thing i could find out about them is that they are injection molded (you can se the injection point).
So, who wants to try them?
